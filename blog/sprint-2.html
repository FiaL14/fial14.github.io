Identity & Values


Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.


Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved
in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.


I'm sure everyone can say that they have been asked for money or spare change at least once in their life.
I know I certainly have, and one particular time in my late teens always sticks out for me. A man outside of the bus stop that
used to be across the road from Area 51 on Cuba Street, stopped me and asked for $10. I obviously didn't know the guy and $10
to me at the time was a lot. Usually I would have said no, but this time was different. I considered that he might use the money
to support a bad habit, or even purchase something illegal. I asked him what it was for and he replied saying he needed it to get
home. I then considered that if he really did need it to get home and I could help him, then why not. Besides, if this guy had
kids or something to get back to then I should help him. After a back and forth conversation I decided, what the heck and helped him out.
I actually gave him $20 because I didn't have anything smaller on me, and I also couldn't be bothered breaking it. After handing him
the money, he shook my hand and said "REALLY?!" and I could see how genuinely happy this dude was. He actually leant in for a hug to say
thanks too, being a big hugger myself I allowed it lol.
After that, I waited with him for the bus, he waved goodbye and that was that. I sat at the bus stop for awhile after he left to think
about what just happened. It was such a random and small act of kindness but for some reason that left a lasting impression on me that
I have never forgotten. Since then I adopted a new motto "If you can help, then help. If you can't, then try your best" which
I have applied in everything that I do. I love helping people out in any way that I can, seeing and hearing the joy in people you've
helped always gives me more meaning. Over the years I've continued to practice this in all of my Customer Service roles and I would now
like to do that as a Web Developer.



Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.


Having grown up in a Samoan home meant a lot of things. I grew up with a strong religious belief which gave me
an understanding of Jesus, which then translated into me learning to be kind, caring and loving towards others. Being able to speak
another language also made me aware that not everyone communicates the same and that each language is unique and not to judge others
in how they speak. Being brown also taught me that not everyone will view me the same, that there are others out there that would judge
me based on my appearance instead of my merit and achivements. This was something I would not completely understand until I was in my
early teens. Once I understood this, I ensured to try to treat everyone I encounter with the same dignity and respect that I would want, and
always try to remember that not everyone has or had the same things that I may or may not have had, so never to judge as it's not within
my right and to be grateful for what I have and try not to take it for granted. Lifes too short and I want to live it up with those I love
and with as many friends as I can make while I'm alive. Living is alive, but living alone is death.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss
the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.


In almost every role I've had working as a Customer Service Specialist, I've had to work in a team. Now that has never required me
to participate in team projects, but it does require us to work together to ensure that we all meet our targets for the month.
In a lot of cases we would need to strategise how we would be as productive as possible so we would meet these targets. On one occasion that I can recall,
I had somewhat of a run in with a colleague about how we should approach the upcoming month as we weren't doing well for that current month. I expressed
one opinion where my colleague expressed another which I didn't agree with. The discussion was going nowhere, it was starting to become awkward for the other
team members and so we needed a solution. In the end I ended up compromising with my colleague providing that we at least implement parts of my idea as a trial,
that way we try both in a sense and if it didn't work try one or the other. In the end we hit our target with that initial plan and all was well in the land.
Upon reflecting on this, I haven't actually done that until now but... If I could replay that whole scenario, I wouldn't exactly fold to the other persons idea,
I'd just suggest trying one idea, if that didn't work out then try mine. And then if neither work, well back tothe drawing board. No harm no foul, well not really.
